About Thingqbator

ThingQbator is a global network of makerspaces for Cisco employees, which was started 4 years ago at Cisco Bangalore office. Now we have branches in San Jose, London, Pune, Dubai and Krakow. Lab is equipped with the latest gear for people to build awesome prototypes in domains such as IOT, Robotics/Autonomous Vehicles, AI/ML and AR/VR.

Journey so far

I joined thingqbator at the very beginning when the program was started. Since then I worked on intelligent robotics solutions and also provided mentorship to teams who joined our cohort (7 cohorts and counting). Me and my team works on various use-cases of UAVs(multirotor) and ground bots (turtle & Heavy duty 4wd). Over time we have demonstrated following features:

  • Autonomous drone with on-the-fly mission upload
  • Vechile control: Mobile app (For user control, live feed, and inventory display) and radio(as usual)
  • Object classification
  • Collision detection and avoidance
  • Pedestrian detection & tracking
  • 2D and 3D SLAM: Path planning and navigation i.e move_base
  • Precision landing mechanism (As GPS has few meters of variance)
  • DJI ROS SDK integration
  • Payload delivery mechanism
  • Integration with Azure
  • Zed and LiDAR integration

These features can be enabled on each platform based on payload carrying capacity. Majorly targeted uses are related to surveillance, medical/package delivery and warehouse management.
The initial drone had features like following a path specified by GPS waypoints, following a specific person/target, live video feed transmission. It took a lots of iterations of development, testing and debugging before a functional drone was ready. Here is one of the several crashes the drone went through. In this mission, drone had to autonomously navigate through a predetermined path. Since it was a cloudy, windy day and there was a lot of electromagnetic interference from huge Cisco Labs , GPS & air speed estimation became noisy. At the end of the mission, drone estimated ground zero at 6 feet above the ground and disarmed itself, finally crashed.



Pick and place mechanism for drones - Medical use case

Drones can be used to deliver drugs to remote areas especially when there is an emergency or a natural calamity like floods. To pick up drugs from warehouse and drop it at another specific location, we developed a solution that lands the drone precisely and also has a robust pick-up and drop mechanism. At first, the drone landing has to happen precisely for which relying only on GPS is not enough. For this we developed a solution combining GPS and image markers. Once the drone lands, to attach the payload to the drone, we developed a ball socket joint system which you can see in the image below. We 3D printed most of the parts required and tested it out in entirety with the drone.
